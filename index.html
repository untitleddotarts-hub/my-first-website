Our Story

Mana Pottery is a family atelier rooted in an ancient pottery village. For three generations we've worked clay by hand, passing down forms, glazes, and the quiet discipline of the wheel. Each piece is a negotiation between intent and material â€” practical, handcrafted forms that carry the traces of the hands that made them.

Craftsmanship & Process

We mix our own clay and glazes from locally sourced minerals. Pieces are wheel-thrown or hand-built, then bisque-fired, glazed, and fired again in an electric kiln. Small-batch production ensures quality control and allows us to offer customization on many pieces.

Design Philosophy

We design for longevity. Our kitchenware is oven- and microwave-friendly, our gardenware is frost-resistant when labeled, and our decorative ceramics are made with archival glazes. We aim to create objects that become part of daily rituals â€” vessels for food, light, and memory.
